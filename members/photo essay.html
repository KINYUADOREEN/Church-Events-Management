Breaking Buckthorn

by Nora Crumley

Earth Day celebration, a generational affair of volunteers ranging from 5-70, pick up trash and remove invasive species at a Cook County Forest Preserve

Sibilings learn about local birds after picking up trash at BackYard Nature Center's Earth Day Clean-Up at the Skokie Lagoons on April 22.

7th grader Serena Vandergrift cuts a piece of buckthorn, an invasive species that replaces native flora and reduces diversity.

Natalie and Dena Sangurima, 13, work together to cut down a buckthorn branch; the volunteers' goal is to remove the invasive buckthorn, allowing other native species to grow.

Daniel Kielson, founder and president of BackYard Nature Center, instructs Ben Wiegand, 17, on how to cut down a large buckthorn tree. "Our mission is to connect children and adults with the wonders of nature," Kielson said.

"I am doing this for extra credit, my French teacher told me to come," Wiegand said.

Jennifer Jenkins and Bob Rudis battle a buckthorn: "Did you come to take a picture of a 60-year-old woman cut down a tree?" Jenkins said.

Exasperation hits as Jenkins and Rudis realize the buckthorn's branches are stuck in surrounding trees.

Rudis plants his feet as he begins to lift the buckthorn out of the entanglement.

John Levine walks past a fire where a stack of cut buckthorn is smoldering; volunteers burn the buckthorn after removing it.

Rudis relaxes on the bench after battling the buckthorn tree. "Gosh, that was tiring," he said.
